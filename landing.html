The Challenge

Minimizing Freight Costs Across a Complex COA Network

International Materials (IMI) operates a network of Contracts of Affreightment (COAs) that govern how bulk cargo is shipped from Spanish origins — Almeria and Garrucha — to 18 discharge ports across the East Coast, Gulf Coast, and Canada.

Each COA group carries contractual obligations: minimum and maximum shipment counts that must be honored per planning period. At the same time, every destination port has a minimum demand that must be met.

The competition objective is to determine the optimal number of shipments per lane per destination — minimizing total freight cost while satisfying all contractual and demand constraints using Integer Linear Programming.

Constraint Structure

Demand Constraints (18)
Column sums ≥ minimum shipments per discharge port
Group Constraints (6)
Row-pair sums between MIN and MAX per COA contract group
Integrality Constraints (216)
All decision variables must be non-negative integers
Big-M Exclusions
Cost = 999 marks impossible routes — automatically blocked
Objective Function
Minimize Σ cost[i,j] × shipments[i,j]
